Chapter 2

The ledger, a first representation of the currency.

For the first times, we will make an analogy between Coinet and a simple Ledger. Imagine that you are sharing some money with other people for daily expenses and some people in the group owes you money meanwhile you owe money to others.

A simple way to organise the exchanges is to have a ledger and note all the transactions in it. For the moment, it may seems strange but a cryptocurrency is a kind of ledger, shared by all the users, with a very high security thanks to some computing technologies. In fact, having a Coinet only means that it is written that you own a coinet.


The problem with this kind of organisation is that a ledger is based on trust between the contributors and it is not possible to rely on that with a money.

Crytocurrencies are just exchanging the trust between the participants with cryptographic technologies in order to secure the Ledger.

The first thing to know about the ledger is that there is no possible overspending, it means that you can not spend more money that you actually have.

During this course, we will teach you the way of working of Coinet, including digital signatures, proofs of work or hash functions.
